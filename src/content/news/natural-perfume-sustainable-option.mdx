Ecologically conscious inhabitants of our little blue dot in space know that we are an unreasonable, merciless, and destructive species that has pushed many others — with whom we share the hospitality of this planet — beyond the brink of survival. And many more species we continue to unreasonably, yet diligently, push towards extinction.

Let us consider how creators of natural perfumes should behave in order to continue their craft with a clear conscience and ensure the sustainability of their practices.

It is important to follow information on the status of plant and animal species from which fragrant materials are produced. Several organisations (e.g.
<a href="https://www.haop.hr/hr/sto-je-cites-i-kako-djeluje" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-accent-600 underline hover:text-accent-800 transition-colors">CITES</a>,
<a href="https://hr.wikipedia.org/wiki/IUCN" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-accent-600 underline hover:text-accent-800 transition-colors">IUCN</a>)
monitor global nature conservation and sustainable use of natural resources, so you can look for information on their websites. I find particularly practical the list published every six months by the <a href="https://airmidinstitute.org/subscribe-to-the-biannual_list/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-accent-600 underline hover:text-accent-800 transition-colors">Airmid Institute</a>,
which you can receive for free by signing up via the link provided. It is available in several languages, including Croatian. The list carefully tracks the endangered status of species, and when deciding on sourcing materials you can safely rely on their assessments. A great relief and convenience!

<div class="bg-gradient-to-r from-botanical-50 to-nature-50 p-6 rounded-2xl border-l-4 border-accent-500 not-prose">
  Let us look at the current situation on the natural fragrant materials market. Globally critically endangered are: agarwood, costus, spikenard, and grey ambergris. These fragrant materials should definitely NOT be sourced, as it is practically impossible to verify the ethical credentials of any particular production — making the decision to use them potentially highly problematic.
</div>

When you have the list in front of you, be sure to also study the National & Regional section to see the status of endangered fragrant materials in various parts of the world. It is good to know the situation closer to your location and your own doorstep.

Caution is also required when deciding to source materials such as vanilla extracts, frankincense, common myrrh, Atlas cedar, sandalwood…

Creating natural perfumes differs significantly from creating commercial, synthetic perfumes. Creators of natural perfumes work with complex materials, not with individual fragrant molecules — the "tools" of commercial perfume creators.

Experienced and competent natural perfume creators who have mastered the skill of making material doubles (analogues, bases) will, with a little thought, scent analysis, and practical experimentation, surely succeed in finding suitable substitutes for materials they cannot use.

Today, an increasing number of consumers are turning to green options. They care about the food they consume, the preparations they apply to their skin, and the products they use to maintain their homes — so it is no longer unreasonable to expect growing interest in natural perfumes.

To the question of whether nature and its resources can support a shift to natural perfumery while respecting ecological standards and sustainability principles, I would be inclined to answer positively! Naturally, this requires a high level of competence from creators and a great deal of care towards the environment. It would be possible! A great challenge is on the horizon.

<div class="bg-gradient-to-r from-botanical-50 to-nature-50 p-6 rounded-2xl border-l-4 border-accent-500 not-prose">
  And finally — homework for natural perfume creators: try to create a scent double/analogue of a vanilla tincture from the fragrant materials you have available! Good luck in finding the combination that satisfies your nose!
</div>
