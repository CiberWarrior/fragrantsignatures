## And it happened! The first exhibition of natural perfumes in Croatia!

At Villa Ružić in Rijeka, despite extremely unfavourable weather conditions, curious visitors gathered — eager to discover how creators of natural perfumes can illustrate characters from *Tales of Long Ago* by Ivana Brlić-Mažuranić. That, indeed, was the task set for the creators: read the book, choose a character they wish to portray through a scent impression, and create a perfume accordingly.

The event was impeccably organised by Prof. Theo de Canziani (with the wholehearted assistance of creative team member Mirjeta Žužić Vranić), custodian of the Mažuranić-Brlić-Ružić literary heritage. The impressiveness of the occasion was greatly enhanced by the Girls' Choir Josip Kaplan under the direction of Maestra Doris Kovačić, and by Prof. Mirjana Kajdi Žužić's monotypes.

So, our senses of smell, hearing, and sight were thoroughly and pleasantly engaged. And to ensure the sense of taste was not neglected, Andrea Furlan provided champagne and cocktails, while Inga Jerkin and Marija Čivčić contributed delicious cakes.

The perfume creators had an extraordinary and wonderful opportunity to explain their creative inspiration and ideas to the curious guests of the exhibition — an experience I believe they will remember for a long time.

  When speaking of natural perfumes created in our region, it is worth noting that we have two creators (Miranda Angelovski Čebron and Martina Godnič) who have registered their perfumes on the European market! To achieve this, the perfumes had to comply with strict IFRA (International Fragrance Association) regulations. So, safer it cannot be!
</div>

The exhibition at Villa Ružić was international in character, as the perfumes presented were created by people from Slovenia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Croatia. It was a superbly organised, unparalleled event that intensely engaged the senses and — despite the cold and rain — brought warmth to the heart and ennobled a Sunday morning!
